Blueberry Ricotta Lemon Cake

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/3 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk (add 1 tablespoon lemon juice to 1 cup milk, let sit for about 10 - 15 minutes in order to make buttermilk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla (optional)

For the Ricotta Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces (1 pack) cream cheese
  • 1 ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • ⅓ cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese

For the Blueberry Jam:

  • 2 cups frozen blueberries (let it come up to room temperature)
  • ½ cup raw honey

Procedures

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F, butter and line cake pans with parchment papers and make sure your butter and eggs are room temperature.
  2. Beat the sugar and butter until it turns pale yellow (almost white). Then crack all your eggs into a seperate bowl (to avoid shells) and mix in one egg at a time. Beat on medium high speed for approximately 8 minutes. Then add in the optional vanilla.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix in all your dry ingredients: flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, lemon zest.
  4. Add the dry ingredients and buttermilk in the egg and butter mixture alternatively and gradually on low speed. Use spatula to scrape down the side occasionally. Once everything is mixed, stop (do not overmix as the gluten in all-purpose flour can develop, resulting in a tough cake).
  5. Pour the cake batter into pre-lined cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes. Let cool completely when done.
  6. To make blueberry jam: mix the honey and blueberries inside a pot and simmer for approximately 20 minutes (until thicken). Let cool completely.
  7. Beat the cream cheese until fluffy. Add powdered sugar, ricotta cheese, lemon juice and lemon zest. Get ready to assemble.
  8. Between the layers of the cake will be both the blueberry jam and the ricotta frosting. Use any combination of the jam and ricotta frosting to decorate the top. And, give yourself multiple slices for that hard work!
